The Process

Your 12-Step Publishing Pathway

From contract to global publication — a clear, structured journey with expert guidance at every stage.

Contracting

Agree dates, sign contract and 50% deposit to secure your publication date.

Manuscript Submission

Submit manuscript with author questionnaire. Reviewed immediately to confirm everything is ready.

Cover Design

Your designer creates 2–3 creative directions. Together you refine until you love the cover.

Cover Sign Off

Approve the final cover design as ready for print and global publication.

Editing

Your editor strengthens clarity and flow. Optional developmental edit for deeper structural guidance.

Proofreading

Professionally proofread and checked by a second proofreader for complete accuracy.

Manuscript Sign Off

Review and approve your final manuscript as ready for typesetting.

Typesetting

Professionally typeset for paperback with optional hardback — it now feels like a real book.

Kindle Formatting

Formatted for Kindle ebook distribution, reading perfectly on all digital devices.

Audiobook Creation

Professional virtual voice audiobook production. Option to record in your own voice.

Publication Sign Off

Review the final typeset book and give approval for worldwide publication.

Global Publication

Published worldwide in paperback, Kindle and Audiobook. Available globally from day one.

The Package

Everything Included

The standard PublishU package covers a 30,000-word book published in three formats — paperback, Kindle and audiobook — everything you need to professionally publish worldwide.

Optional enhancements include hardback, human-voice audiobook, extra word count, premium developmental editing, illustrations, infographics, language translation, and 60-day fast-track publication.

Community

The Global Authors Club

When you publish with PublishU, you become a lifetime member of an international community of 100+ authors from around the world. Regular online gatherings, shared ideas, and a private author community — because building the impact of a book should never be done alone.
